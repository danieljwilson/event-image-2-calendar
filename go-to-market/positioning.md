# Positioning & Messaging

## Core Messaging

**One-liner:** "Snap a poster. Get a calendar event."

**Tagline:** "Photos to calendar, instantly."

**Elevator pitch (30 sec):**
You see a concert poster, a campus flyer, or an Instagram event — and you want it on your calendar. Instead of typing it all in manually, just snap a photo or share the link. Event Snap uses AI to extract the title, date, time, and venue, then creates a calendar event in one tap. It works from any app on your phone via the share sheet.

**Problem statement:**
Adding events to your calendar from physical posters, flyers, or social media posts requires manually copying details — title, date, time, venue — into a calendar app. This friction means events get forgotten or never make it to your calendar.

## Target Audiences

### Primary: College students and young professionals (18-30)

**Profile:** Attend lots of events — concerts, meetups, campus events, food festivals, bar nights. See event posters on bulletin boards, Instagram stories, and group chats constantly. Calendar is their lifeline for staying on top of a packed social schedule.

**Pain point:** "I saw a poster for this show last week but forgot to add it to my calendar, now it's sold out."

**Where they are:** Instagram, TikTok, Reddit, campus Discord servers, Eventbrite, Facebook Events.

**Messaging angle:** Never miss an event again. Snap the poster, done.

### Secondary: Parents managing family schedules (30-45)

**Profile:** Juggling kids' school events, activity schedules, sports games, community events. Get paper flyers sent home in backpacks, emails with event details, community board postings.

**Pain point:** "My kid brought home three flyers this week and I still haven't added any of them to the family calendar."

**Where they are:** Facebook groups, school/parent apps, local community forums, Pinterest.

**Messaging angle:** Paper flyer to family calendar in seconds.

### Tertiary: Conference and event professionals

**Profile:** Attend or organize multiple events. Need to quickly capture event details from promotional materials, competitor events, industry calendars.

**Pain point:** "I'm at a conference and there are 20 side events — I need them all on my calendar now."

**Where they are:** LinkedIn, Twitter/X, Eventbrite, industry Slack communities.

**Messaging angle:** Batch-capture event details from any source.

## Competitive Landscape

**Direct competitors:** Few — most calendar apps focus on scheduling, not event capture. Some OCR-to-calendar apps exist but lack semantic understanding (they read text, not event context).

**Indirect competitors:**
- Manual calendar entry (the default)
- Google Lens (can read text but doesn't create calendar events)
- Eventbrite/Meetup apps (only for their own events)
- Apple Live Text (extracts text but no calendar integration)

**Positioning:** Event Snap is not a calendar app — it's a calendar *input* tool. It sits between discovery (seeing an event) and commitment (adding it to your calendar), eliminating the friction in between.
